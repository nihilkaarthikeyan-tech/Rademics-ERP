1 Two separate doors

Everyone does not log into the same place. There are two websites.

Internal App (Staff)Your team logs in here — HR, PMs, employees, finance, and the super admin. Full ERP.
|
Client PortalOnly clients log in here. A small, safe window that shows just their own projects.
Why this matters: a client can never wander into your HR, salary, or attendance screens. They only ever reach the portal, and the portal only ever shows them their own projects.

2 The 7 roles & what each one does

Every person is given one role. The role decides what they see and what they can do.

Super Admin

Owner / Top

The boss account. There should be very few of these — ideally just you.

Their job
  • Controls the whole system and its settings.
  • Creates clients and gives them access (this is the key onboarding step — see section 4).
  • Assigns roles to staff (only Super Admin can change who is HR, PM, etc.).
  • Can view the audit log — a record of every important action anyone took.
Features they have
  • Everything. Finance, HR, projects, client management, settings, audit log.

HR

Staff

Handles people — hiring, records, attendance rules, leave, payroll.

Their job
  • Adds and edits employees, manages departments, deactivates people who leave.
  • Views salaries, sees everyone's attendance, sets attendance & leave policies.
  • Approves leave and can export payroll.
Features they have
  • People directory, attendance (all staff), leave policies, payroll export. No project or client-portal control.

PM — Project Manager

Staff

Runs the actual client work. The main bridge between the client and the team.

Their job
  • Creates and edits projects, closes/archives them.
  • Creates tasks and assigns them to team members.
  • Reviews finished work and decides what the client is allowed to see.
  • Marks files "client-visible" — this is how a client actually receives a deliverable.
Features they have
  • Full project & task control, file uploads, reports, expenses on their own projects.

Team Lead

Staff

A senior employee who leads a small team under a PM.

Their job
  • Creates and assigns tasks within their own team/projects only.
  • Reviews their team's work, approves their team's attendance fixes and leave.
  • Does their own tasks too.
Features they have
  • Team-level view of projects, tasks, attendance, and leave — scoped to their team, not the whole company.

Employee

Staff

The team member who does the hands-on work.

Their job
  • Checks in/out for attendance, requests leave, requests attendance corrections.
  • Works on tasks assigned to them and updates their own task status.
  • Uploads their work files and comments on tasks.
Features they have
  • Their own tasks, their own attendance/leave, projects they're part of, the AI assistant. They can't see salaries, finance, or other teams.

Finance

Staff

Handles money — invoices, payments, expenses, payroll.

Their job
  • Creates invoices, records payments, logs expenses.
  • Views profit & loss and exports payroll.
  • Can see their own salary record.
Features they have
  • The full finance area. No HR editing, no project control.

Client

External

Your customer. Logs into the Portal only — never the staff app.

Their job
  • Watches the progress of their own project(s).
  • Downloads files your team has marked as client-visible.
  • Views their invoices.
  • If given Approver access: approves a deliverable or asks for a revision.
Features they have
  • A read-only window into just their projects, files, and invoices — plus approve/revise if they're an Approver. Nothing else in the company is visible to them.

3 How login works (same for everyone)

Nobody sets their own password from scratch or self-registers. They are invited, then they set a password. This is true for staff and clients alike.

  1. An account is created (invite)An authorised person creates the account with an email, a name, and a role. The account starts as “Invited” — it cannot log in yet.
  2. An email is sent automaticallyThe person gets a “You've been invited to Rademics ERP” email with a Set your password link. The link expires in 7 days.
  3. They set their passwordThey click the link, choose a password (minimum length + must contain a number). The account becomes “Active”.
  4. They log inFrom now on they log in with email + password. Staff links point to the Internal App; client links point to the Portal — the system picks the right door automatically based on the role.
Built-in safety: after too many wrong passwords the account locks for a while (and an email warns them). Forgot-password sends a fresh reset link. Changing a password logs the person out of all devices.
In short: Invite → email → set password → log in. That's the whole login story.

4 Bringing a client in — the "client id" & login

This is the part you asked about most. A client is not one loose account — first you create a Client Organization (think of this as the client's “id” / their company folder), then you invite people into it, then you give them access to specific projects.

1. Create Client OrgThe client's identity in the system
→
2. Invite client userA login is created inside that org
→
3. Grant project accessViewer or Approver on a project
→
4. Client logs into PortalSees only what you allowed
  1. Super Admin creates the Client Organization Only the Super Admin can do this. You give it a name (e.g. “Acme Ltd”). This org is the client id — everything about that client (their people, their projects) hangs off this one org. Names must be unique, so no two client orgs can clash.
  2. Super Admin invites a client user into that org You add the client's contact person by email + name. The system creates a login with the Client role, tied to that org, and emails them the “set your password” link (same invite flow as section 3). You can invite several people from the same client company into the same org.
  3. Super Admin (or PM) grants project access A client login on its own shows nothing yet. You attach the client user to a specific project with one of two levels:
    • Viewer — can watch progress, download client-visible files, and see invoices.
    • Approver — everything a Viewer can do, plus approve a deliverable or request a revision.
    The first time you grant access, that project is locked to this client's org, so it can never accidentally show up for a different client.
  4. The client logs into the Portal They open the Client Portal, log in with email + password, and see only their own projects — the ones you granted, nothing else. No staff data, no other clients.
Turning a client off: if you deactivate a Client Org, every login inside it is instantly signed out and blocked. Clean “access ended”, one click.
Simple mental model: Org = the client. Client user = a person at the client who can log in. Project access (Viewer/Approver) = which projects that person is allowed to watch. Every action here is recorded in the audit log.

5 How the client and your staff talk to each other

Contact happens inside the portal, around the work itself — not by giving the client your team's phone numbers or internal chat. Everything stays tied to the project and is logged.

What happensStaff sideClient side
Sharing a deliverable PM/staff upload a file and mark it “client-visible”. The file appears in the portal for the client to download.
Approving work Team submits a deliverable for the client to sign off. An Approver client clicks Approve, or Request Revision with a reason.
Discussion Staff comment on tasks; client comments come back on the shared items. The client can leave comments on the work they can see.
Progress updates As staff update task status, progress moves automatically. Client watches live progress — no need to ask “where are we?”.
Money Finance issues invoices against the project. Client views their invoices in the portal.
Key rule: a client only ever sees a file if a staff member deliberately marked it client-visible. Nothing leaks by accident — the default is private.

6 How your staff get information about a client

Staff don't need the client's login. They see the client through the project.

  • PM & Team Lead: open the project to see who the client is, what's been shared, what the client approved or sent back, and the client's comments.
  • Employees: see the project and tasks they're assigned to. They see the client's requests and comments on those tasks, but not the client's private account details.
  • Finance: sees the client through invoices and payments on that project.
  • The link is the project: a client is joined to a project, staff are joined to the same project, so both sides meet on that shared project — each seeing their own allowed slice.
One line to remember: the project is the meeting point. Client on one side, staff on the other, and the portal decides exactly what crosses over.
