Section 1 · Overview

One platform to run the whole agency

Rademics ERP covers the full operating loop — people & roles, attendance & productivity, projects & tasks, client collaboration & approvals, leave, and finance & invoicing — with reporting and an AI layer on top. Because everything lives in one system, work flows straight through: an employee finishes a task → a client approves it → Finance invoices it → the payment appears in profit & loss. No spreadsheets or email chains in between.

Section 2 · Capabilities

Everything we built — area by area

Eight connected areas. Each lists, in plain terms, the concrete things it does.

01

People & HR

Every staff member and freelancer in one directory, with the organisation structure behind it.

  • Full profiles: photo, contact, department, team, reporting manager, join date, skills, documents
  • Salary information kept encrypted and visible only to those allowed
  • Freelancers: pay-per-deliverable terms, contract & NDA files, never in attendance/leave
  • Structure: Department → Team → Member, one Team Lead per team
  • Departments map to verticals (Publications, Design, Web, Support) for profit reporting
  • Offboarding logs the person out, reassigns open tasks, keeps all history
02

Attendance & productivity

Trustworthy time data — designed to drive payroll without disputes.

  • Check in / out from desk or phone; multiple sessions a day supported
  • Daily total = sum of sessions; worked vs. idle time shown in real time
  • Nothing tracked invisibly — idle minutes are always visible to the person
  • Late, half-day and overtime worked out automatically each night
  • Correction requests route to the Team Lead / HR to approve
  • Forgotten sessions auto-close at midnight; live “who’s online now” view
03

Projects, work streams & tasks

The delivery engine — with a strict, recorded review flow so nothing skips a step.

  • Structure: Project → Module → Task → Subtask, with priorities, estimates, deadlines
  • Work streams for continuous work (support, moderation), measured by weekly throughput
  • Board (kanban), list and calendar views; filter by person, status, priority, deadline
  • Comments with @mentions, kept separate for internal vs. client-visible
  • Budget per project, visible only to managers and Finance
  • Every status change records who, when and why — and can never be edited
04

Client portal

A separate, secure app where clients follow work and sign off — without email chains.

  • Each client user sees only granted projects, at milestone level with % complete
  • Two access levels: Viewer (see & download) and Approver (also approve / request revision)
  • Internal task details, staff names and internal files are never visible
  • Approve or request a revision (with comment) from the portal — the PM is notified
  • See their own invoices and payment status
  • Notification style per user: real-time, daily digest, or weekly digest
05

File management

Safe file handling with full history and virus scanning.

  • Every upload is a new version — nothing is overwritten; full history kept
  • Each file is marked internal or client-visible; changing that is recorded
  • Every upload is virus-scanned before anyone can open it; infected files quarantined
  • Images and PDFs preview in the browser; other files download
  • Files attach to tasks and to staff/freelancer profiles
  • Upload limit and handling configurable by the admin
06

Leave management

Balances, requests, approvals and a shared calendar — with the policy built in.

  • Leave types with quotas: Casual, Sick, Earned (carry-forward), Unpaid
  • Balances accrue monthly and are always visible, including projected accrual
  • Request with type, dates (half-day supported) and reason → routed up the chain
  • Unactioned requests escalate automatically after 48 hours
  • Team leave calendar with an overlap warning when approving
  • Leave beyond balance auto-converts to unpaid and flags into payroll
07

Finance & invoicing

The full money side — invoices, payments, expenses, profit, and payroll export.

Invoice life: Draft→ Sent→ Partially Paid→ Paid· Overdue (auto)
  • Invoices against a client & project, with line items — description, quantity, rate, GST %
  • Branded PDF, emailed to the client and shown in their portal; numbered RAD-YYYY-####
  • Payments recorded against invoices; partial payments supported
  • Invoices go Overdue automatically after the due date; reminders sent
  • Dues view per client with ageing (0–30 / 31–60 / 61–90 / 90+ days)
  • Expenses logged per project (freelancer payouts, tools) with receipts
  • Profit & loss per vertical: revenue minus expenses, any date range
  • Payroll export: monthly CSV of payable days (loss-of-pay applied), for Tally/Zoho
08

Reports, AI & audit

Dashboards for every role, four AI helpers, and a tamper-proof record of sensitive actions.

  • Attendance, productivity, project-status and finance reports — export CSV/PDF
  • Every role has a tailored dashboard; every number clicks through to the list
  • Reports are scoped — a Team Lead sees their team, a PM their projects
  • Four built-in AI helpers (detailed in Section 6)
  • Audit log: append-only record — no one can edit or delete it
  • Skills & capacity view so work goes to real available bandwidth

Section 5 · How it works

The life of a task — from created to closed

Every piece of work follows the same controlled path. It moves one step at a time, and the system records who did what and when. Sending work back or requesting a revision always needs a written comment.

1
Draft
A manager or team lead creates the task and assigns it.
→ becomes “Assigned”
2
Assigned
The person it's given to acknowledges they've picked it up.
Assignee → “Acknowledged”
3
Acknowledged
They start the work.
Assignee → “In Progress”
4
In Progress
Work happens; draft files are uploaded, then submitted for review.
Assignee → “Submitted for Review”
5
Submitted for Review
A manager approves it — or sends it back with a comment. Client-facing work goes to the client next; internal work is done.
Manager → “Client Review” or “Completed”
6
Client Review
The client opens the deliverable and either approves it or requests a revision with a comment.
Client → “Completed” or back to “In Progress”
7
Completed
Finance marks it invoiced; the manager then closes it. (Internal tasks can close without an invoice.)
Finance → “Invoiced” → Manager → “Closed”
8
Closed
The task is finished and locked, with its full history preserved.
End of the line

Section 6 · In practice

Everyday workflows

Employee A working day

  1. Log in and check in (desk or phone)
  2. Open My Tasks Today, acknowledge a new task
  3. Start work, upload a draft, submit for review
  4. Get a “sent back” note, fix it, resubmit
  5. Check out — tomorrow's summary ready next morning

Leave Request & approval

  1. Employee opens My Leave, picks type & dates
  2. Shows as pending; balance checked automatically
  3. Team lead (or HR) approves inline
  4. Employee notified; balance updates
  5. Unactioned requests escalate automatically

Client Approving a deliverable

  1. Client gets a “deliverable ready” email
  2. Logs into the portal → Approvals
  3. Previews the file and its versions
  4. Approves, or requests a revision with a comment
  5. Progress view updates for everyone instantly

Finance Invoice to payment

  1. Completed work appears in the ready-to-invoice queue
  2. Finance creates a branded invoice (GST applied)
  3. Sends it — client sees it in portal & email
  4. Records payments; overdue invoices flag automatically
  5. Once paid, profit & loss reflects it

Four AI helpers — always within a person's own access

The assistant can never show someone data they couldn't already open themselves, and every AI answer is labelled AI-generated.

Daily summary

A one-click end-of-day recap for a manager: what each member completed, what's in progress or blocked, plus attendance anomalies.

Completion forecast

Predicts a project's finish date and flags deadline risk with the reason — e.g. “the review stage is the bottleneck”.

Assignment suggestion

Suggests the best people for a task, ranked by skill and current workload. The human always makes the final pick.

Ask-anything assistant

A chat panel to ask about your own work — “what's overdue on Project X?”, “who's free this week?” — with sources cited.

Built-in across the whole system

Security & access

  • Invite-only accounts — no public sign-up
  • Locks for 15 min after 5 failed logins
  • Password reset links expire in 30 minutes
  • Staff and client sessions fully isolated

Notifications

  • In-app (real-time) and email for key events
  • Assignments, approvals, @mentions, leave, invoices
  • Deadline reminders 24h before, and when missed
  • Each person chooses in-app, email, or mute

Audit trail

  • Logs logins, permission & salary changes, exports
  • Shows who, what, before/after, when, from where
  • Append-only — no one can edit or delete it

Skills & capacity

  • Skill tags on every profile
  • Open work vs. weekly capacity, traffic-light
  • Assign against real free bandwidth
